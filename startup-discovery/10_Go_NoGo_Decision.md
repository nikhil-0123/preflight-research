# Executive Summary

# Major Risks

## Risk 1

### Market Consolidation

Large vendors may expand into agent governance.

Examples:

* Microsoft
* Google
* CrowdStrike
* Palo Alto Networks

Risk Level:

Medium

Mitigation:

Focus on innovation and research rather than immediate commercial competition.

---

## Risk 2

### Agent Adoption Rate

Enterprise adoption of autonomous agents may progress slower than expected.

Risk Level:

Medium

Mitigation:

Design the platform to support:

* AI agents
* LLM applications
* Multi-agent systems

rather than a single framework.

---

## Risk 3

### Technical Complexity

Behavioral analysis and anomaly detection are challenging.

Risk Level:

High

Mitigation:

Begin with:

* Rule-based monitoring
* Policy enforcement
* Audit trails

Add ML components incrementally.

---

# What Should NOT Be Built

Avoid:

* Generic SOC Platform
* Generic Chatbot
* Generic AI Agent
* Full Enterprise SIEM
* Autonomous Penetration Testing Platform
* Large-Scale Threat Intelligence Platform

These require resources beyond a student team.

# Final Verdict

## GO

Proceed with TaskAnchor.

However:

Do NOT attempt to build a complete commercial AI security platform.

Instead:

Build a focused, technically strong MVP centered on:

* Agent Monitoring
* Runtime Governance
* Goal Drift Detection
* Behavioral Risk Analysis

Use the project to maximize:

* Technical learning
* Research opportunities
* Recruiter value
* Startup optionality

while maintaining realistic execution goals for a 3-member engineering team.
